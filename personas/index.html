Introduction

The RIPTA (Rhode Island Public Transit Authority) bus system is the premiere public transit system of Providence, Rhode Island. To fund it, the city collects a small fee from each rider as soon as they step onto a bus. For a class assignment in CSCI 1300: User Interfaces / User Experiences at Brown University, I developed a storyboard and two personas based on my observations and interviews of riders interacting with the payment system to understand it better while practicing some of the basic methods of user interface design.

The RIPTA Payment System

The RIPTA payment system affords riders seven distinct methods of instant payment when they step onto the bus. A rider can either scan their RIPTA-issued "WAVE" card in one of two ways via the card scanner, pictured right, or employ one of five alternative methods of payment using the rectangular metal device, pictured center.

Observations & Interviews

Over nearly an hour of riding the RIPTA bus, I observed just over a dozen riders interact with the payment system, and interviewed three.

Key Observations

  • No matter how they paid, stepped onto the bus with their card or cash in hand.
  • The typical rider spent no more than 2-3 seconds paying.
  • Of the seven methods of payment available to riders, cash took the longest.
  • Most riders paid for the bus using a WAVE or Brown University ID card.
  • Riders knew their payment went through once the payment system beeped.

My Interviews

I asked the following set of six questions to three riders:
  1. Why are you taking the RIPTA today?
  2. When you saw the RIPTA approaching, did you do or think anything to prepare to pay?
  3. How did you expect the interaction to go when you saw the RIPTA approach?
  4. What actually happened? Did your interaction change from how you expected it to go?
  5. So I saw that you used <payment method>.
    1. How did you know that your payment went through?
    2. Why <payment method>? Why not...?
  6. Is any part of the payment experience ever difficult, frustrating, or stressful for you?
1
  • Takes the RIPTA because they have a disability that prevents them from driving.
  • Gets a free pass from RIPTA because of their disability.
  • Expects the RIPTA payment experience to go smoothly every time.
  • Knows that the payment went through because of a beep sound.
  • Has never experienced any substantially difficulty besides trying to fit dollar bills into the machine once. Never again!
2
  • Takes the RIPTA as a Brown University student to get to and from the Whole Foods on Waterman Street.
  • Gets their Brown University ID card out as the RIPTA approaches to swipe.
  • Expects the payment experience to go smoothly after the initial confusion around how to swipe their ID.
  • Knows that the payment went through because of a beep sound.
  • Gets stressed out whenever they have a big line of people getting on the bus behind them.
3
  • Taking the RIPTA today to visit a friend.
  • Doesn't report doing or thinking anything to prepare for the RIPTA as it approaches.
  • Expects the RIPTA payment experience to go smoothly every time.
  • Prefers to scan their WAVE card over other payment methods because they can easily "load it up" on their phone every now and then.
  • Never has had any notable frustrations or difficulties with the RIPTA payment experience.

Personas
